What is Quantum Computing?

Quantum computing is a new paradigm of computing which uses physical quantum phenomena to drastically speed up certain computations. Unlike classical computers that rely on bits, which can be either 0 or 1, to process data, quantum computers use qubits which can be any value between 0 and 1, as well as complex values. This allows for much faster computations on problems such as factoring large numbers, which is a key part of modern encryption.

Applications

Computer Science

One of the most famous quantum algorithms to date is Shor’s algorithm, which allows us to factor large numbers incredibly fast. Though this seems to have little use, much of modern encryption relies on the inability to factor these numbers - quantum computers change that.

Chemistry

Quantum computing can be used to simulate other quantum processes much more accurately than classical systems. This could prove helpful in certain tasks, such as developing new drugs.

Physics

One of its most profound applications lies in quantum simulations, where quantum computers can mimic the behavior of quantum systems. This capability transcends classical computing limits, enabling the precise study of molecules, materials, and particles at the quantum level. By harnessing quantum bits or qubits and their inherent superposition and entanglement properties, researchers can unlock insights into the intricate dance of particles and forces that govern our universe.
